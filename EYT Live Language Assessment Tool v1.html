Scoring Rubric — Four Dimensions
Fluency (FL) Pacing, filler words, planning pauses, flow continuity
Linguistic Range (LR) Vocabulary breadth, syntactic complexity, idiomatic use
Phonological Control (PC) Intelligibility, accent impact, prosody and stress
Grammatical Accuracy (GA) Verb tenses, subject-verb agreement, sentence structure
How Qualifying Level Works
The qualifying level is the lowest dimension score across all four categories — not an average. A candidate who scores C1 on three dimensions but B1 on Grammatical Accuracy qualifies at B1.

B1 — Internal support; limited external communication
B2 — Customer-facing, team lead, account coordinator, HR generalist
C1 — Senior client-facing, manager, trainer, quality analyst
C2 — Executive, international-facing, senior client management
