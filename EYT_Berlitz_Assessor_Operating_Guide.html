Before you startSave your API key

Settings only has one key to manage now: Anthropic, which powers all four dimension scores (Linguistic Range, Grammatical Accuracy, Fluency, Phonological Control). There's nothing Deepgram-related to paste anymore — Recall.ai runs that on its own end for Live Interview Mode.

  1. Open the Settings tab.

    Paste your Anthropic key into the field under Anthropic (Scoring), then click Save for Session.

    This only lasts for the current browser tab session — reload the page and you'll need to paste it again, unless you use the "Embed Key in File" option below to download a personal copy with it baked in.

ⓘ

The key never leaves your browser except to call Anthropic's API directly — there's no backend involved in scoring itself.

Two ways to scorePick the right mode

No live audio

Transcript Upload

You already have a written transcript. AI scores Linguistic Range and Grammar from the text; Fluency and Pronunciation need your ear, so they're marked N/A for the AI and left to you.

Real-time, via Google Meet

Live Interview Mode

A bot joins your actual Google Meet call and scores all four dimensions as the interview happens, including pronunciation — no manual audio review needed afterward.

Mode 1Transcript Upload

  1. Fill in candidate details and paste or upload the transcript.

    Name, role, department, and assessor name, plus the transcript text itself.

  2. Click Assess.

    The AI returns Linguistic Range and Grammar scores with evidence quoted from the transcript.

  3. Add your own scores for all four dimensions.

    Every row now shows an AI score next to an Interviewer dropdown — including Linguistic Range and Grammar, if you want to weigh in on top of the AI. Fluency and Pronunciation are yours to judge; the AI has no audio to work from here.

  4. Click Update Suitability once Fluency and Pronunciation are scored.

    Regenerates the final write-up using all four dimensions instead of just the two AI ones.

    Whichever score you enter as Interviewer takes priority over the AI's for the final qualifying level — the AI score is only the fallback if you leave a dimension blank.

  5. Download the PDF, or add the candidate to Batch Tracker.

Mode 2Live Interview Mode

This sends a real bot into your Google Meet call — it'll show up to the candidate and interviewer as a participant named EYT Assessor.

⚠

Tell the candidate first. A bot visibly joining and recording the call may need disclosure or consent depending on where the candidate is located. Check with whoever handles compliance before rolling this out widely.

  1. Fill in Candidate Information.

    Candidate name, role, department, assessor name.

  2. Paste the Google Meet link into the field under Recording.
  3. Click Start Interview.

    The bot is sent into the call — this takes a few seconds. Status updates appear above the transcript panel while it connects.

    If the backend has been idle a while, the very first request can take 30–60 seconds to wake up (free-tier cold start) — this is expected, not a failure.

  4. Let the interview run.

    The transcript fills in below the scoring panel as people speak. Linguistic Range, Grammar, and Fluency update roughly every 90 seconds; Pronunciation is scored once, at the end, from the call's word-confidence data.

  5. Check that speaker tagging is right.

    Candidate vs. Interviewer is detected automatically from the Meet participant names. If it's backward, click 🔄 Swap — this corrects the entire transcript and every score computed so far, not just what's said from that point on.

  6. Click Stop & Score when the interview ends.

    The bot leaves the call and final scores are generated for all four dimensions.

    Needs at least 30 words of candidate speech captured — if the interview was too short, you'll be told to run a longer one instead of getting a score.

  7. Review the Qualifying Level and add your own scores.

    Same as Transcript Upload — every dimension shows both an AI pill and an Interviewer dropdown, including Pronunciation and Fluency, which the AI has already scored here (unlike Transcript Upload).

  8. Click Update Suitability, then download the PDF or add to Batch Tracker.

Tracking multiple candidatesBatch Tracker

Every candidate you add (from either mode) lands here in one table — AI and Interviewer score per dimension, qualifying level, and the suitability write-up.

  1. Download Batch (.xlsx) for the full spreadsheet.

    One summary sheet with everyone, plus a detail sheet per candidate with the full AI and Interviewer notes for all four dimensions.

Worth knowingKnown limits

⚠

Not yet tested against a real call. The backend's mapping of Recall.ai's data — who's speaking, and the word-confidence numbers behind the Pronunciation score — is a best-effort guess at the payload shape. Run one real test interview before trusting this for an actual candidate, and flag it if speaker names or Pronunciation evidence look off.

⚠

Billing is shared. Every Live Interview across every assessor draws from the same Recall.ai account and API key. Keep an eye on usage once more than one person is running interviews.

ⓘ

The backend's free tier spins down when idle. For a real interview where timing matters, consider the paid tier (~$7/mo) so Start Interview doesn't stall on a cold start.
